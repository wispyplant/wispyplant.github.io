My conversation with renowned science fiction scholar and author Grace L. Dillon is now published in Revisions: Vol 1 by re:arc institute. Grace 'is best known for coining the term Indigenous Futurism, which is a movement consisting of art, literature and other forms of media which express Indigenous perspectives of the past, present and future in the context of science fiction and related sub-genres.' We talked about power, narratives, collaborations and much more. 

The conversation took place online in June 2023 and was moderated by Monica Uszerowicz. 

re:arc institute works 'at the intersection of climate action and architecture philanthropy, we fund community-led solutions that address the root causes and consequences of climate breakdown.'
